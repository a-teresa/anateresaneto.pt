AuraScope

        
An Auracast BLE-audio receiver that enumerates as a USB sound card on a Zynq-7000 (Arty Z7-20), running a custom Yocto image — with the audio pipeline made observable from inside the kernel (a char driver I wrote) and from outside (eBPF). Built and documented as a public series of small, shippable steps.

About

      // who's at the keyboard
    

    

      

        I'm a firmware / embedded engineer focused on making constrained
        hardware do reliable, useful things — from BLE audio on nRF53 to
        Yocto-based Linux on ARM.
      

      

        In my current role I work on Bluetooth applications on the nRF5340 with a
        focus on real-time audio transmission — BLE and BLE audio across UART, USB,
        SPI and I²S on Zephyr RTOS in C/C++ — alongside Yocto-based embedded Linux.
        Earlier I worked across the full IoT stack: bare-metal and FreeRTOS firmware
        for STM32F4/F7, customized embedded Linux images with Yocto, and the systems
        built around them — including a C++ access-control service on embedded Linux
        tying together MongoDB change streams and Kafka, plus the web and cloud
        deployment behind it. I've also shipped FOTA over SMP, a custom ESP32 mesh,
        and UWB ranging on DW3000-class modules. I hold an MSc from Instituto
        Superior Técnico and have a published paper on running multilabel anomaly
        detection on microcontrollers. I care about real-time guarantees, clean
        board bring-up, and BSPs that the next engineer can actually maintain.
